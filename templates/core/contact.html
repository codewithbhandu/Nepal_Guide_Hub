Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about Nepal travel

Most tourists need a valid passport and a tourist visa. Tourist visas are available on arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport or can be obtained from Nepalese embassies abroad. You'll also need proof of sufficient funds and a return ticket.

The best time depends on your activities. For trekking: October-November and March-May offer clear mountain views. For wildlife safaris: October-March is ideal. Monsoon season (June-September) brings lush landscapes but challenging trekking conditions.

While independent trekking is possible for some routes, we highly recommend hiring a licensed guide for safety, cultural insights, and navigation. Guides are mandatory for certain restricted areas and can enhance your overall experience significantly.

You can browse packages on our website, contact agencies directly, or use our contact form. We'll connect you with verified agencies that match your requirements. All bookings are made directly with the travel agencies, and we provide support throughout the process.

All our partner agencies are verified and licensed. They follow strict safety protocols, carry appropriate insurance, use experienced guides, and maintain emergency communication equipment. We regularly audit our partners to ensure they meet our safety standards.
